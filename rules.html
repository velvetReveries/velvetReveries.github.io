Rules

Community, In-Game, and Conflict Guidelines

Community Rules & Info

1.1 General

  • Don’t be a dick, be respectful of others in the community. This includes our staff.
  • Do not preach hate within the community.
  • Communities develop a sense of what is right and what is wrong, take the time to learn. Don’t expect your sense of humor or right and wrong to match the communities, adapt.
  • This is a North American based server. Our primary language is English and you must be able to communicate in English.
  • Streamers are welcome here so be aware of the Twitch ToS and Rules of Conduct and also familiarize yourself with the Discord Community Guidelines.
  • This is an 18+ server. Failure to tell the truth will result in a permanent ban.
  • Erotic Role-Play (ERP) is Prohibited. Grotesque or gruesome scenarios must provide a warning to players.
  • In scenes which may contain questionable graphic content (i.e. suicide, self harm, gore) all participants must be warned OOC prior to its beginning, and given the chance to opt out.
  • You must be present in the Discord to be in the Server.
  • Adjust your name in Discord to include your character name. (Ex.: Franklin the Hermit | BreakerOneNine)
  • Abusing loopholes, falsifying evidence, leaving Discord etc. to get what you want will result in punishment.
  • Rule breaking with evidence must be submitted through the ticket system.
  • If it concerns staff, please direct it to Glyph RP Management through an HR Ticket or an Anonymous HR ticket.
  • We cannot regulate community discords.
  • Please remember to be courteous in your voice RP. Constant loud screeching into your mic, animal noises, or blaring sounds can cause other players discomfort and be seen as griefing.

1.2 Discord

  • Do not directly message staff with Glyph RP or in-game related questions. You must create a ticket or ask in our appropriate help channels.
  • Do not tag staff in your tickets.
  • Do not spam chats.
  • Do not post or link to mature, explicit, controversial, political or racist content (including anything depicting pornography or gore).
  • OOC chats are for Out-of-Character content and IC chats are for In-Character content.
  • Ravens & Radios is an in-character message system for basic communication. Treat it like a carrier pigeon!
  • Make healthy choices, do not join chats or post while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.
  • The Meeting Channel is used for Community Meetings.

1.3 Disclaimer

Do not message server administrators directly for general questions. We have helpful community channels in Discord where you can ask for assistance. If this is not an issue that can easily be resolved by the community, then please Submit a Ticket. If you have an issue with another member of staff, please message senior management immediately.

Staff can and will enter your tribe at any time for routine checks or for Player Assistance/Progression. Any staff member reserves the right to perform Game Administrator duties without explanation to the public or affected parties.

All staff may flag you for rule violations. Our Enforcement Team’s job is to handle player reports, create/amend rules and handle day to day enforcement/check operations. All staff are trained and know the rules thoroughly to handle rule violations.

It is a player’s responsibility to read all rules and seek clarification if needed. All rules are subject to change at any time at the administrative team’s discretion.

1.4 Strikes

  • There is a 3 strike system of punishment.
    • Strike 1 – A warning will be sent.
    • Strike 2 – A ticket or meeting with the Enforcement Team.
    • Strike 3 – Ban – Temporary or Permanent Depending on Severity.
  • Each season, one strike will fall off of you; however, you will not be unbanned if you are permanent banned.
  • Continued failure to follow the rules can and will result in a permanent ban.
  • Depending on the severity and results of staff investigation may result in an immediate ban.

ARK General Rules

2.1 Character

  • Characters must be legal adults within the race parameters, both physically and mentally.
  • If a character has already whitelisted with the name you have chosen, you will be asked to add a surname or title.
  • Do not use special characters in your name.
  • Do not role-play famous people or characters.
  • The whitelisting team has every right to deny a name or character.
  • You must look like your chosen race.
  • You may only switch Guilds, Jobs, or Magics once per character a season.
  • No pregnancy role-play.

Wounded Character Rules

When a character is killed in an RP significant instance and requires their wounds to be tended through RP.

  • An RP significant instance includes a Glyph event, a PVP instance, or a situation involving staff NPCs.
  • If you are wounded in a PvP encounter, the nature of the wounds are decided by the victor. Permanent wounds need to be agreed upon by both parties.

2.2 Taming

During Taming:

  • Once another player has begun taming, the creature is claimed. Do not disrupt the process or kill it.
  • Guarding a wild dino for another player does not grant it taming rule protection until the process begins.

Tamed Creatures:

  • Tribes may only own up to 200 + 100 per person tames.
  • Maximum 35 creatures deployed per tribe at any one time.
  • Creatures must be passive unless enclosed in a fully constructed building.
  • Do NOT tame Territory creatures unless you are part of that Territory.
  • Clean up taming traps after you're done.

2.3 Building

  • Tribes of one-or-two players are allotted a 1000 structure footprint. An additional 250 structures per additional active member.
  • Max size for any one build is 15 structures in any dimension.
  • All rooms must have a door.
  • No more than two buildings on rafts or platform saddles (max 8x4x3).
  • Do not encase a build in planter terrain.
  • Beds are only allowed at homesteads/outposts. Temporary beds for dungeons/raids are fine but must be removed.
  • Fences or walls must be no higher than four walls.
  • Building within epic render distance of Glyph builds and other player’s settlements is not allowed without permission.
  • Objects may not appear floating or unsupported.
  • Building near rich resource/role-play/important areas is not allowed.

2.4 Role-play

  • No meta-gaming (using OOC knowledge IC).
  • No Power-gaming (maximizing progress over storytelling).
  • No God-modding (controlling other players' characters).
  • No Fail RP (breaking RP rules, going OOC).
  • Your character must value their life.
  • Do not bleed/blur IC and OOC interactions.
  • Warn participants OOC before graphic content.

Character Death:

  • Force permadeath is not allowed.
  • Open a ticket for a new character and post a graveyard post.
  • One reroll per four weeks.
  • Do not pass on old gear/creatures to your new character (heirlooming).
  • If you are the only person in your tribe you must tear down builds before rerolling.
  • Clear bank accounts and turn in honor coins before rerolling.

2.5 Guilds & 2.6 Jobs

There are no current rules for guilds and jobs at this time.

2.7 Magics

Mysterium

  • Do not share your spells with other players.
  • Do not allow other players to interact with your Nexus book.

Shamans

  • If you fall below 50% HP in a PvP scenario or RP scene while in form, you must see an Alchemist.

Revenants

  • Joining the Revenants just to 'out' the identities of fellow Revenants is not permitted.
  • If you are injured as a Revenant, you must still see an Alchemist for healing.
  • Give OOC warnings for descriptions involving gore during feeding roleplay.
  • For a person to be a willing food source, they must consent both IC and OOC.

ARK Conflict & PvP Rules

3.1 Conflict Roleplay

Conflict RP is a popular avenue of RP. Glyph sets precedents to ensure conflicts respect our rules and community intent.

  • CONTENT: Heinous or villainous actions may result in content loss (removal from guild, job, magic).
  • LOSS OF RP: Villainous behaviors may result in a character being unwelcome in some groups.
  • UNPLANNED IMPACT: Consequences may ripple past intended targets.
  • MITIGATION SYSTEM: Expect IC actions to have NPC responses.
  • In any conflict, all parties agree to adhere to Glyph rules. A rulebreak does not justify another rulebreak.

3.2 PvP

Glyph is a roleplay server first. PVP is not a focus, but we set boundaries to minimize griefing.

PvP Rules and Standards

  • Combat must be initiated through IC RP with "//PVP INTENT" and an "//PVP ACCEPT" from the other player(s).
  • A player may also "//PVP YIELD" to forfeit, or "//PVP ACCEPT FLEE" to try and escape.
  • If a player crashes/disconnects, disengage. Do not interact with or steal from 'sleeping players'.
  • Logging out or using carriages to avoid conflict is not allowed.
  • Do not kill passive creatures unless they are used to attack.
  • Tools may not be equipped upon creatures that may use them in PvP.

Concluding PVP Encounters

A PVP encounter ends when:

  • Player/s have been mechanically killed.
  • A player/s surrenders.
  • A player/s have been tranquilized AND handcuffed.
  • 10 minutes have passed without engagement.
  • A fleeing player loses line of sight for 1 minute.

Attacking on Sight (AoS)

  • Random-Death-Matching and Kill-on-Sight (RDM/KOS) is not allowed.
  • AoS is only allowed if agreed to by all players in a ticket with clear terms.
  • Events, Glyph towns, and public transportation are never valid spaces for AoS.

3.3 Crime

Crime should be used to promote roleplay, not to grief or harass.

  • Before committing crimes, you must open a thieving ticket and have a "Calling Card" approved by staff.
  • Leave your Calling Card and one other piece of evidence (physical evidence, clothing fiber, biome-specific item).
  • Do not cause excessive damage. "One way in, one way out."
  • Do not take more than 3 items and no more than ⅓ of any gold found.
  • After a theft, you cannot return for criminal activities for 72 hours.
  • Do not steal soul-trapped creatures or fertilized eggs.
  • Breaking into a home constitutes a PVP Accept for the thief if the owner catches them.

3.4 Escalating to War

Only a tribe or territory leader may start the war process by opening a ticket. Both parties must agree to escalate to war.

Declaration of War

The declaration must include:

  • ANNOUNCEMENT: The IC portion.
  • PARTIES INVOLVED: A list of all attackers and defenders.
  • TERMS OF VICTORY: Demands and win/loss conditions.

3.5 War

Territory leaders will decide on the number of battles (max 3), with one battle per week. There are five rulesets to choose from for battles, which can be customized with agreement.

Rulesets for Warring:

  • CHAMPION BATTLE: 1v1 battle between representatives.
  • LAND BATTLE: Ground-based team battle.
  • SEA BATTLE: Water-based team battle on ships or with sea tames.
  • AIR BATTLE: Air-based team battle with flying tames.
  • OBJECTIVE BATTLE: Team battle focused on capturing or destroying a specific objective.
